# Publishing Checklist

Use this checklist before moving a post from `_drafts/` to `_posts/`.

## Content quality

- The post documents real execution rather than generic theory.
- The opening makes the problem and stakes clear.
- The body explains decisions, tradeoffs, and outcome.
- The conclusion captures a useful lesson, result, or next step.

## Metadata quality

- Required front matter fields are present.
- `category`, `subcategories`, and `tags` follow existing conventions.
- `type` reflects the value of the post rather than the topic alone.
- `summary` is short and specific.
- `featured` and `pin` are used intentionally.

## Public safety

- No secrets, tokens, credentials, cookies, or internal-only notes are present.
- Screenshots, logs, and diagrams are safe for a public repository.
- Internal hostnames, private IPs, and sensitive identifiers have been removed.

## Presentation quality

- Headings are clean and ordered.
- Code blocks are readable and relevant.
- Images, diagrams, and tables add signal rather than noise.
- The post reads well on both desktop and mobile.
